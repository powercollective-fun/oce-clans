The History of OCEClans
A 2026 Journey Phase 1: The Vision
In early 2026, the Minecraft SMP scene was dominated by servers that felt either too cluttered or too restrictive. Hauntd saw an opportunity to build a "back-to-basics" Java server that prioritized performance. The name OCEClans was chosen as a tribute to the community spirit we wanted to foster—a place where groups of friends could settle together and build their own legacy without the need for complex "factions" plugins or forced combat mechanics.

Phase 2: Technical Excellence
We spent the first few months of 2026 exclusively on hardware optimization. We didn't want a server that lagged the moment someone built a simple iron farm. By investing in dedicated Ryzen 9 hardware, we ensured that OCEClans could handle dozens of players simultaneously exploring the world without dropping a single tick. This technical foundation is what allows us to host massive community events and large-scale builds today.

Phase 3: Community First
Since our launch, we have focused on one thing: the player. We don't believe in "pay-to-win" models. Every feature on OCEClans is designed to be accessible to everyone who plays. Our economy is entirely player-driven, and our world is shaped by the houses, shops, and roads built by you. We are proud of the friendly atmosphere we've created, where players help one another rather than tearing each other down.

Phase 4: The Future
Looking ahead into the rest of 2026 and beyond, we plan to continue expanding our world border as new Minecraft versions are released. We remain committed to being a Java-exclusive server, ensuring that we never have to compromise our quality for the sake of cross-platform compatibility. Your adventure on OCEClans is just beginning, and we are honored to have you as part of our story.
Server Rules
1. No Cheating The use of hacked clients, X-Ray texture packs, or any third-party software that gives an unfair advantage is strictly forbidden. We value the integrity of the survival experience. If you are caught cheating, you will be permanently banned without the possibility of an appeal.
2. No Griefing Respect other players' work. Do not destroy, modify, or steal from builds that do not belong to you. This includes stealing items from chests or killing player pets. We have extensive logging tools to track every block change; you will be caught.
3. Toxicity We aim for a friendly and inclusive environment. Racism, sexism, homophobia, or any form of harassment will result in an immediate mute or ban. Be kind to your fellow players and keep the chat civil and helpful for everyone.
4. Lag Control Avoid building massive redstone machines or entity farms that negatively impact server performance. If your build is causing the server TPS to drop, a staff member will ask you to optimize it. We want a smooth experience for all players.
5. Advertising Do not promote other Minecraft servers, Discord communities, or external products in our public chat or private messages. We are here to play OCEClans, not to see advertisements for other services.
6. Exploits Using glitches to duplicate items, bypass world borders, or gain invincibility is a ban-able offense. If you find a bug, please report it to Glaze or Hauntd immediately so we can fix it.
Survival FAQ
Q: How do I protect my house?
A: We recommend using bases like End, Nether, If you use overworld bases you could have a chance of getting base found quicker because the overworld is where most players are.

Q: Can I teleport to my friends?
A: Yes! You can use `/tpa [playername]` to request a teleport to a friend. They must accept the request for you to move. You can also set a home using `/sethome` to return to your base at any time.

Q: Is PvP allowed?
A: PvP is enabled in the wilderness, but we discourage "player hunting" or killing people who do not want to fight. Always ask before engaging in combat.

Q: How do I earn money?
A: You can earn money by finding diamonds, emeralds, and trade with villagers. We don't use an econonmy system just normal modded minecraft..

Q: What version should I use?
A: We are currently running on 1.21. We always recommend using the latest stable release of Minecraft Java Edition to ensure you can see all the new blocks and items correctly.

Q: Can I use shaders or mods?
A: Client-side mods like Optifine, Iris, Sodium, and various shader packs are highly encouraged! They make the server look beautiful. Just ensure you are not using "cheat" mods like X-Ray or Auto-Clickers.

Q: What happens if I die?
A: When you die, your items will stay at your death location for 5 minutes before despawning. We recommend having a `/sethome` or a bed nearby so you can run back quickly to retrieve your gear.

Q: How big is the world?
A: Our world border is currently set to 20,000 blocks in every direction from 0,0. This gives everyone plenty of room to explore and find the perfect spot to build their base.

Q: Are there ranks I can buy?
A: We offer cosmetic ranks that provide cool chat tags and particle effects. These are entirely optional and do not give any gameplay advantage. Check our store link in Discord for more info.

Q: How do I report a grief?
A: If you find that your base has been messed with, do not touch anything! Open a ticket in our Discord or message a Moderator in-game immediately. We can check the logs and rollback the damage instantly.
